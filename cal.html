Converting all-grain to Extract

Number of Lbs of Grain X 0.75 efficiency = Lbs. of extract

Example: 9 lbs. of two row grain = ? lbs of extract

9 lbs. X 0.75 = 6.75 lbs. extract

Determining your IBU (International Bittering Units):

Hops weight in ounces = IBU's desired / (3.5 x hop alpha acids)

Example: 35 IBU's desire using fuggles hops ( 5.1% alpha acids )

35 IBU's / ( 3.5 x 5.1 Alpha acids) = 2 oz. fuggles hops

Note: IBU's are approx. because of factors that may change the release of alpha acids. (i.e. boiling time and heat). The longer that you boil your hops the more Alpha acids that will be released. Also if you brew at a low boil, it may take longer to release alpha acids

To determine how much malt to use for a (specific) Specific gravity:

((SG -1 )(Gallons of wort desired)) / (Extract rating - 1) = Lbs. of malt needed

Example: For 15 gallons of 1.040 wort desired with malted barley

((1.040 - 1)(15 gallons)) / (1.028 - 1) = 21.4 Lbs. of malt needed
